Children's Rights in India

Right to Education:

Every child has the right to free and compulsory education up to the age of 14 years under the Right to Education Act.

Protection from Child Labor:

The Constitution of India prohibits the employment of children under the age of 14 in hazardous occupations.

Health and Nutrition:

Children have the right to health and nutrition. Government programs aim to provide immunization, nutrition, and healthcare services.

Protection from Abuse and Exploitation:

Laws such as the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act aim to protect children from sexual abuse and exploitation.

Right to Identity:

Every child has the right to a name and nationality. Birth registration is crucial to ensure this right.

Right to Play and Recreation:

Children have the right to play and participate in recreational activities for their holistic development.

Special Protection for Minorities and Weaker Sections:

Special provisions exist for the protection and upliftment of children belonging to minorities and weaker sections of society.

Adoption Rights:

Legal provisions govern the adoption process, ensuring the welfare of children and protecting their rights.

Right to Expression:

Children have the right to express their views in matters affecting them, as per the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC).

Protection from Trafficking:

Laws are in place to prevent child trafficking and protect children from being exploited in various forms.

Child Welfare Institutions:

There are institutions and agencies responsible for the welfare and rehabilitation of children in need of care and protection.
